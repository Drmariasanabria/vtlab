BRIDGE COMMAND

An interactive oral training session for Maritime English in the professional sphere. 6 stations · ~2 hours · Captain–Officer dynamic with required oral interaction.

▸ Voyage Progress — Bridge to Engine Room

— Incoming transmission from Naviera Internacional —

You are the Junior Officer aboard the MV Phonetica, a 220-metre dry cargo vessel preparing for departure. The Captain (your instructor) will guide you through six operational stations across this watch. This is a spoken simulation: most tasks require you to speak aloud, and the Captain will validate, correct or escalate your output.

⚓ The Six Stations

  • 01 · Bridge Radio — IMO Standard Marine Communication Phrases (SMCP). VHF calls.
  • 02 · Cargo Manifest Review — read, reformulate and report on cargo documentation.
  • 03 · Emergency Drill — random crisis events; you explain procedure orally under pressure.
  • 04 · Letter Desk — analyse complaint and application letters; dictate improved drafts.
  • 05 · Voyage Briefing — describe routes, weather, hazards orally to the bridge team.
  • 06 · Captain's Debrief — integrated final exchange, written report on the watch.
⚠ RULES OF THE WATCH
  • Fuel (your time budget, 120 minutes) decreases as the simulation runs. Random events can refuel or drain it.
  • Knots are your points. The faster and more accurately you complete tasks, the more knots you earn.
  • Reputation is your streak with the company. Consecutive APPROVE validations build it; REPEAT or ESCALATE resets it.
  • The Captain (instructor) validates every spoken task from the bottom panel.
  • Random events trigger every 4–7 minutes: radio calls, port complaints, weather alerts, mayday signals — react fast.
  • Power-ups are at the Captain's discretion: hints, anchor (pause), almanac (vocabulary).
